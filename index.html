The earth is about 2.6 billion years old. Until now, at least four major ice age periods are identified, the last started about 2.5 Mya and lasts until now.

Pleistocene ice ages

Changes in

  • Ice cover
  • Sea level
  • Ocean currents
  • Ocean gateways
Since the last ice age period started, temperature swung in the Pleistocene (a geological epoch) from warm to cool conditions in a regular pattern. The LGM (started 20,000 ya). At this time, glaciers covered the northern hemisphere and trapped so much sea water that the sea level was about 130m lower than nowadays. Moreover ocean currents changed and ocean gateways to the Arctic closed. Oxygen-isotope method. 18O/16O isotope ratios depend on temperature. Its ratio in fossil shells thus indicates the temperatures of the water they existed in. In the deep sea it was due to glacier coverage. 16O evaporated more easily in glaciers and left a higher proportion of 18O in the seas.

A recent review showed also how important seagrasses are really for fishes of commercial importance. Cod, for example, which lives during adulthood rather in deeper waters, finds at its juvenile stage food and protection in seagrass meadows but also in kelp forests.
the assembly and function of a rich wood web that supports fishes and other economically exploited marine species is well visualized here in the kelp forest along the east Pacific coast where we see invertebrates sheltered by the kelp blades, invertebrates using the three-dimensional structure of the blades as habitat to grow on, fishes, that feed on the invertebrates living around the kelp, and bigger mammals that feed on invertebrates and the fishes.

Carbon sequestration

61-268 Tg C per year

Krause-Jensen & Duarte (2016); Nature Geoscience

Air bladders are common among brown algal taxa and facilitate their long-range transport (i). Langmuir circulation forms windrows of macroalgae (ii) and can force the algae to depths where water pressure makes the air bladders burst and the algae then sink. Macroalgal carbon can be sequestered either via burial in the habitat or by transport to the deep sea where it is sequestered whether buried or not (iii). About 90% of this sequestration occurs through export to the deep sea, and the rest through burial in coastal sediments.

What climate-change related factor has the highest impact?

Seaweed distribution limited by 10°C summer and 20°C winter isotherm
We can approach this question by looking at which environmental factor is most important in setting distribution limits. For seaweed, it is well known that their distribution is limited by certain isotherms (line of equal temperature on a geographic map) to the north and the south.
Identifying most important range-limiting factors
We wanted to identify out of a range of environmental factors which of them are most important for the distribution of seaweed. Because we wanted to know how the distribution of a range of macroalgae will change under projected climate change. We did this with an approach called ecological niche modelling. Here the model allows to evaluate out of a range of environmental factors, which one can predict the presence and absence of the species best.
Temperature as most important factor

Jueterbock et al. (2013,2016); Ecology and Evolution

For three of the dominant temperate species and the most dominant intertidal species in the arctic, sea surface temperature and surface air temperature derivates were the most important factors.
